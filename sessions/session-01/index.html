Getting Started with Scratch

Welcome to your first Scratch session! Today you'll create your first interactive animation.

Your First Script

Here's a simple script to get you started:

when green flag clicked
say [Hello, DG!] for (2) secs
move (10) steps

Challenge: Make a Sprite Dance

Try combining these blocks:

when green flag clicked
repeat (4)
  move (10) steps
  turn right (90) degrees
end

Quick Tips

  • Use the green flag to start your program
  • Red stop sign stops everything
  • Right-click blocks to duplicate them
  • Drag blocks to delete them

Facilitator Notes

Teacher Goal

Ensure logins work, establish collaboration protocols, and surface SDG curiosities.

Artifacts

  • team charter
  • starter project link
  • account verification

Flow

  1. Story spark (5 min)

    Share a quick SDG story and ask: “What could we build in Scratch to explain this?”

  2. Studio tour (15 min)

    Walk through stage, sprites, blocks, and backpack. Students complete a scavenger hunt.

  3. Mini challenge (20 min)

    Build a “Hello, DG!” animation that changes backdrops and uses at least one event + one motion block.

  4. Team charter (15 min)

    Assign rotating roles, draft communication norms, and list personal Scratch goals.

Teacher Moves

  • Circulate with a debugging protocol: ask “What do you expect this block to do?” before giving hints.
  • Capture any access issues (logins, hardware) for remediation before Session 2.

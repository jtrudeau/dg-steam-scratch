First Build Sprint

Today has three parts: get your Google Doc up to date, build in Scratch, and demo what you have.

By the end of today:

  • Your team's Google Doc has everything it needs
  • You've built something in Scratch
  • Every team gives a one-minute demo of what they have so far
  • Teams that didn't present in Session 06 present what they didn't get to present last time

Step 1: Google Doc Check

Before you build anything, open your team's Google Doc and make sure it has all of the following. If something is missing, add it now.

Confirm you have:

  • Team name
  • All team members listed
  • Business idea — a clear description of what your business does
  • SDG connection — which UN Sustainable Development Goals does your business connect to, and how?
  • Scratch project idea — what are you building in Scratch? What does the user do?
  • Scratch project link — a working link to your team's Scratch project
  • All team members have edit access to the Google Doc
  1. Go through the checklist above.
  2. If anything is missing, update your team's Google Doc now.
  3. When everything is checked, move on to Step 2.

Step 2: Build

Open Scratch. Build.

Some starting points depending on your project:

  • If your project starts with a title screen: create the backdrop, add a sprite with your project name, make it respond to a click or key press to start.
  • If your project is a game: get your main character moving first. Arrow keys, mouse — whatever your blueprint says.
  • If your project tells a story: set up your first scene. Backdrop, characters in position, first line of dialogue or action.
  • If your project asks questions: create one question, get the answer, and do something with it (change a variable, switch a scene, show a response).

Stuck? Raise your hand. Mr. Joel or an intern will come to you. But first — tell us what you tried.

Step 3: Demos + Presentations

Save your Scratch project.

Every team gives a one-minute demo. Show us your screen. Tell us what you have done and what you are hoping to do.

Here is a suggested script:

"Our project is ___. So far we have built ___. When you open it, you can ___. Next, we are hoping to ___."

For example:

"Our project is a quiz game about the lives of blind people. So far we have built a title screen and the first question. When you open it, you can click start and answer a question about daily challenges blind people face. Next, we are hoping to add more questions and a score tracker."

It's ok if you are just getting started.

Facilitator Notes

Teacher Goal

Get every team's Google Doc current with basic requirements for the team hub. Then build time, ending with one-minute demos and any presentations that didn't happen last session.

Artifacts

  • Scratch project with first build element working
  • Google Doc update (what we built, what's next)

Flow

  1. Google Doc check (10 min)

    Every team opens their Google Doc and confirms they have the basics: team name, all members, business idea, which SDGs they connect to, Scratch project idea, and a working Scratch project link. If anything is missing, fix it now.

  2. Build time (25 min)

    Open Scratch. Build. If you're stuck, raise your hand — Mr. Joel or an intern will come to you.

  3. Demos + presentations (15 min)

    Every team gives a one-minute demo of what they have built so far.

Teacher Moves

  • Start with the Google Doc check — no building until the doc is current. Walk the room and verify. Most teams are missing their SDG connection.
  • During build time, circulate with one question: 'Show me what you have so far.' Don't solve problems — ask what they've tried first.
  • Watch for teams that are stuck on scene setup (backdrops, sprites) vs. teams that jumped straight to code. Both are valid entry points — name that for them.
  • For demos: every team shows what they have, even if it's just a backdrop and one sprite. The point is momentum, not polish.
  • Reassure teams that are early in their build — the demo is about sharing where you are, not showing a finished product.

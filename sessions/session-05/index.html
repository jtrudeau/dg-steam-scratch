Meeting 5

Team Home Base Setup

Finalize your team's project info, create a shared Google Doc, and share your Scratch project.

Sharing ProjectsProject Setup

Your Team Home Base

Today you set up everything your team needs to work together for the rest of the project.

By the end of today, your team will have:

  • A Google Doc with your team name, members, sustainable business idea, and Scratch project ideas
  • A shared Scratch project link in your Google Doc so everyone can find your team's work

Step 1: Finalize Your Worksheet

Look at what you wrote last session. Make sure you have:

  • Your team name
  • Names of every team member
  • Your sustainable business idea (1-2 sentences about the problem you're solving)
  • At least two ideas for how this could become a Scratch project

Step 2: Create Your Team Google Doc

One person on your team creates a new Google Doc.

In the doc, write:

  1. Team Name
  2. Team Members (everyone's name)
  3. Our Sustainable Business Idea — What problem are you solving? How does it help people, the planet, or both?
  4. Our Scratch Project Ideas — your brainstorm from the worksheet

Share the doc with every team member so everyone can edit it.

Step 3: Share Your Scratch Project

Your team needs one Scratch project that everyone can find. If someone on your team already started a project, use that one. Otherwise, create a new one.

How to share a Scratch project:

  1. Open your project in the Scratch editor
  2. Click the orange Share button at the top of the page
  3. Give your project a clear title (use your team name)
  4. The link in your browser bar is your project link — it looks like scratch.mit.edu/projects/123456789
  5. Copy that link and paste it into your team's Google Doc

Good to know:

  • Once a project is shared, anyone with the link can see it and remix it
  • Only the project owner can edit the original — teammates can open it to see the code and remix their own copy
  • Your team will build on this project together over the coming weeks

Step 4: Check In

When your Google Doc has everything — team info, business idea, Scratch ideas, and the project link — call Mr. Joel over to your team. He'll check that you're all set.

Step 5: Explore and Build

Once you're checked in, start building! Try making a small piece of your project idea in Scratch:

  • A character or sprite
  • A background or scene
  • One interaction (click, keypress, movement)
Facilitator Notes

Teacher Goal

Get every team's core info captured and shared. Establish the Google Doc as the team's living document and the Scratch project link as the shared workspace.

Artifacts

  • team Google Doc
  • shared Scratch project link

Flow

  1. Finalize your worksheet (5 min)

    Review what you wrote last session. Make sure you have: team name, all members, your sustainable business idea, and at least two Scratch project ideas.

  2. Create your team Google Doc (10 min)

    One person creates a new Google Doc. Title it with your team name. Add: team name, names of all members, your sustainable business idea, and your Scratch project ideas. Share the doc with every team member.

  3. Share your Scratch project (10 min)

    Pick the Scratch project your team will build on. Share it so teammates can see it, then copy the link into your Google Doc.

  4. Check in with Mr. Joel (5 min)

    When your Google Doc is ready with everything in it, call Mr. Joel over to your team so he can check your setup.

  5. Explore and build (15 min)

    Start building a small piece of your project idea in Scratch. Try a character, a background, or one interaction. Save your work.

Teacher Moves

  • Circulate during Doc creation to catch teams that are stuck on sharing permissions.
  • Verify each team's Google Doc has all required info before they move to Scratch exploration.
  • Note: you can create a class studio later to collect all team projects in one place.
